FAQ

Common questions

Does WordLens work on every website? +
Yes — WordLens is injected into every page you visit, including news sites, academic papers, PDFs opened in Chrome, and web apps. The only exceptions are Chrome's own internal pages (like chrome://extensions) and the Chrome Web Store, which extensions aren't allowed to access.
Is my browsing data sent anywhere? +
Only the hovered word and its surrounding sentence (up to 300 characters each side) are sent to the Anthropic API to generate a definition. No page URLs, no personal data, no browsing history. If you use BYOK, that API call goes directly to Anthropic's servers — WordLens has no backend of its own.
What happens after I use my 50 free lookups? +
WordLens automatically falls back to the free dictionaryapi.dev for standard definitions — the extension never goes silent. An upsell banner appears in the tooltip offering the $2.99 Pro upgrade (+500 lookups) or the option to use your own Anthropic API key for unlimited free lookups.
What's the difference between Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus? +
All three are Claude models made by Anthropic, ranging from fast-and-cheap to slow-and-powerful. Haiku 4.5 costs ~$0.0004 per lookup and is excellent for word definitions — quality is indistinguishable from Sonnet for this task. Sonnet 4.6 (~$0.002) offers stronger reasoning and is great for dense academic text. Opus 4.7 (~$0.008) is the most capable model available, ideal for highly technical or ambiguous terms. Model selection is only available to BYOK users.
How do I cancel or get a refund? +
There's nothing to cancel — WordLens is a one-time purchase with no subscription. If you're unsatisfied within 7 days of purchasing Pro, email us and we'll refund you in full, no questions asked. BYOK users pay Anthropic directly and should manage their usage at console.anthropic.com.
